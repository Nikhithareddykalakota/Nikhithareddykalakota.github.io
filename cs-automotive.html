01. Overview

The Challenge

In-home repair was a completely new service offering, there was no existing flow to reference or iterate on. Customers browsing a services page had no way to request a technician for larger electronics like TVs, refrigerators, or washers. The ask: design and build the entire experience from scratch.

This meant creating three interconnected SPAs: a service landing page, a product-to-issue-to-schedule booking flow, and internal service management pages, all while meeting WCAG 2.1 AA standards and coordinating across engineering, data, and business teams.

02. Process

How we approached it

01

Discovery & requirements

Collaborated with data analysts and developers to define scope for a net-new service. No prior flows existed, requirements were built from stakeholder interviews, competitive analysis, and early user research into how customers sought appliance repair.

02

Persona & journey mapping

Translated research into personas (frustrated appliance owner, time-pressed household manager) and customer journey maps that exposed friction, users calling support or searching third-party repair sites.

03

Wireframing across surfaces

Produced low-fidelity wireframes in Figma across desktop, mobile, and touch device breakpoints. Applied progressive disclosure, the multi-step booking flow was a key structural decision that reduced perceived complexity.

04

High-fidelity prototyping & iteration

Built interactive prototypes in Figma and Adobe XD. Ran structured critique sessions with cross-functional teams, incorporating feedback loops that drove a 30% increase in reach through improved clarity and reduced drop-off.

05

WCAG audit & ADA compliance

Conducted comprehensive WCAG 2.1 audits across all surfaces. Keyboard navigation, ARIA labeling, screen reader compatibility, and contrast ratios, embedded from design, not added after.

06

SPA development & web graphics

Designed and developed all three single-page applications alongside the engineering team. Also produced web graphics and iconography for the in-home repair pages, handling both design and implementation fidelity.

Key Contributions

What I owned

SPA Architecture

Designed and built all three single-page applications from wireframe to production, service landing, booking flow, and service management.

WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

Owned the accessibility audit and remediation, keyboard nav, ARIA labels, screen reader testing, and contrast across all surfaces.

Data-informed Decisions

Regular syncs with data analysts surfaced real user behavior early, grounding design decisions in evidence rather than assumption.

Cross-surface Design

Designed desktop, mobile, and touch breakpoints simultaneously, ensuring a consistent experience regardless of device.

05. Reflections

What I learned

Designing for a net-new service is different

There was no existing pattern to iterate on. Starting from a blank canvas meant leaning heavily on competitive analysis and user research to ground early decisions, and staying close to stakeholders to avoid building in the wrong direction.

Cross-functional alignment accelerates design

Regular syncs with data analysts surfaced real user behavior early. Developer conversations flagged technical constraints before they became rework. The design improved because of those conversations, not despite them.

Accessibility baked in from the start is cheaper

Designing WCAG-compliant patterns from the wireframe stage, rather than auditing at handoff, led to cleaner component architecture and fewer revision cycles. ADA compliance isn't an audit at the end; it's a design constraint from day one.

Structured feedback loops compound

Establishing a rhythm of critique and iteration, rather than one big review, meant problems were caught small. That compounding improvement is what drove the 30% reach increase over the course of the internship.

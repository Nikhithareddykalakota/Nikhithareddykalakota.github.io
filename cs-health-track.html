Overview

Health data is broken.
We're here to fix it.

The idea for Health Track was sparked by a real experience: a friend struggling to gather her medical records from India to share with doctors in the US. Unlike in centralized healthcare systems, patients navigating international or multi-provider care face a fragmented nightmare of paper files, scattered PDFs, and disconnected digital portals.

Health Track was designed to solve this: a single, secure platform where users can aggregate records from any provider, anywhere in the world, with intelligent insights that travel with them.

🎯 The Core Problem

Health records are siloed across multiple hospitals, apps, and paper files with no unified view
International patients face critical gaps when moving between healthcare systems in different countries
Existing apps offer generic, one-size-fits-all recommendations that don't account for individual health context
Patients have no ownership or control over their own health data
Scheduling, records, reminders, and insights require multiple apps, creating friction and drop-off
DESIGN GOAL
Build a single health companion that centralizes records, delivers personalized guidance, and travels with users across borders and healthcare systems.
Research & Insights

Listening before designing

Interviews and surveys with users across varying healthcare backgrounds, including international students, chronic condition patients, and healthcare professionals, revealed five critical insights that shaped the design.

🗂️

Fragmented Records

Users juggle paper reports, email attachments, hospital portals, and pharmacy records with no single place to see their complete health picture.

🌍

Cross-Border Gaps

International users, especially students, arrive with no digital records. Hospitals in many regions don't provide portable or digitized patient data.

💡

Generic Advice Fails

Generic health apps offer the same advice to everyone. Users want insights tailored to their actual labs, vitals, and medical history.

⏰

Time is Precious

Users don't have time to manually organize data. They need automation that respects their privacy and preferences.

🔐

Privacy is Non-Negotiable

Users are cautious about health data. Transparency, encryption, and user control are critical trust drivers.

📱

Mobile-First is Essential

Medical records access happens on the go. A responsive, offline-capable mobile app was the only way to meet user needs.

Organized Health-conscious Detail-oriented

Design Solutions

How we solve the problem

🗂️

Unified Record Hub

A central, searchable repository for all medical records, regardless of source—hospital portals, doctor uploads, or manual imports—accessible anytime, anywhere.

🔐

Privacy by Design

End-to-end encryption, granular sharing controls, and transparent data practices give users full ownership and confidence in their sensitive health information.

📊

Smart Insights

Machine learning identifies patterns in personal health data, offering actionable, contextual recommendations tailored to individual history and goals.

🗓️

Integrated Scheduling

Built-in appointment booking connects users with trusted providers, eliminating the need for third-party calendar apps.

🤝

Doctor Collaboration

Secure sharing links let users send verified records to new doctors without duplicating manual uploads.

📱

Mobile-First Experience

Optimized for on-the-go access with offline support, push notifications for appointments, and a lightweight interface.

Reflection

What I learned

This project reinforced the power of listening to real user pain points and iterating on empathy. The most valuable insights came not from industry research alone, but from conversations with friends managing their health across continents and doctors frustrated by siloed patient data.

Designing for healthcare also taught me the importance of trust and transparency. When users are sharing sensitive medical information, every design decision—from color choices to data handling—communicates intent. Simplicity became essential; unnecessary features only added friction to an already stressful experience.

Most importantly, I realized that great product design doesn't live in a vacuum. Health Track exists to solve a human problem—giving people control and peace of mind about their own health. That mission informed every screen, every interaction, and every technical decision.

1

2

In healthcare, data protection is not just compliance—it's a core value that drives user trust and adoption.

3
Simplicity scales

Removing unnecessary complexity made the app accessible to users across different tech literacy levels.

4
Mobile-first is essential

Health emergencies and appointments happen on the go; desktop-first thinking would have missed critical use cases.
